Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam


Former Precident of India


Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs.



Here's a time line of Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931- Born in Rameswaram
  • 1958- joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO)
  • 1969- He moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation, where he was project director of the SLV-III the first satellite launch vehicle that was both designed and produced in India
  • 1982 - Kalam planned the program that produced a number of successful missiles, which helped earn him the nickname “Missile Man.” Among those successes was Agni, India’s first intermediate-range ballistic missile, which incorporated aspects of the SLV-III and was launched in 1989.
  • 1992 - Kalam was scientific adviser to the defense minister,
  • 1999- he later served as principal scientific adviser (1999–2001) to the government with the rank of cabinet minister.
  • 1998- His prominent role in the country’s 1998 nuclear weapons tests solidified India as a nuclear power and established Kalam as a national hero, although the tests caused great concern in the international community.
  • 1998 - Kalam put forward a countrywide plan called Technology Vision 2020, which he described as a road map for transforming India from a less-developed to a developed society in 20 years.
  • 2002 -Kalam easily won the election and was sworn in as India’s 11th president, a largely ceremonial post
  • 2007 - He left office at the end of his term in 2007 and was succeeded by Pratibha Patil, the country’s first woman president.
  • 2015 - dies in Shilong.
